# Ideation Portal - User Personas

## Overview
The Ideation Portal serves three primary user personas with distinct goals, workflows, and success criteria. This document defines each persona to guide user story development and ensure all user perspectives are represented.

---

## Persona 1: Emma - The Innovative Employee

**Role:** Product Manager / Individual Contributor

**Background:**
Emma is a mid-level employee with 5+ years of experience in her organization. She's tech-savvy, creative, and regularly thinks about process improvements and new ideas. She's motivated by recognition and the opportunity to contribute to organizational success.

**Goals:**
- Submit innovative ideas that could improve processes or create new opportunities
- See her ideas fairly evaluated by knowledgeable panel members
- Receive recognition for high-quality ideas
- Collaborate with colleagues on shared ideas
- Track the progress and impact of her submitted ideas

**Pain Points:**
- Worried ideas might be overlooked or unfairly evaluated
- Uncertain about evaluation criteria and scoring methodology
- Wants to know how her ideas compare to others
- Concerned about idea ownership and credit
- Frustrated when good ideas don't get implemented

**Technical Proficiency:** High - Comfortable with web applications, dashboards, and data visualization

**Success Criteria:**
- Successfully submits ideas with clear descriptions
- Receives fair, transparent evaluation
- Sees ideas ranked on leaderboard
- Gets recognized for top-performing ideas
- Understands why ideas were or weren't selected

**Key Workflows:**
1. Submit new idea with title, description, and expected impact
2. Save draft and return later to complete submission
3. Add colleagues as contributors to shared ideas
4. View idea on dashboard and leaderboard
5. See evaluation scores and panel feedback
6. Receive recognition badge if idea wins
7. Track implementation status of winning ideas

**Motivations:**
- Recognition and visibility for contributions
- Opportunity to influence organizational direction
- Collaboration with peers
- Clear feedback on idea quality
- Seeing ideas implemented

## Persona 2: Marcus - The Evaluation Panel Member

**Role:** Senior Manager / Subject Matter Expert

**Background:**
Marcus is a senior manager with deep expertise in his domain. He's been selected as a panel member to evaluate ideas based on his knowledge and judgment. He takes this responsibility seriously and wants to ensure fair, consistent evaluation.

**Goals:**
- Evaluate assigned ideas fairly and consistently
- Understand evaluation criteria clearly
- See how other panel members scored ideas
- Provide constructive feedback on ideas
- Ensure high-quality ideas are identified
- Maintain evaluation integrity and transparency

**Pain Points:**
- Uncertain about evaluation criteria and scoring methodology
- Worried about bias or inconsistency in evaluation
- Wants to see how other evaluators scored before finalizing own score
- Concerned about evaluation timeline and workload
- Needs clear visibility into evaluation progress

**Technical Proficiency:** Medium - Comfortable with web applications, may need guidance on complex features

**Success Criteria:**
- Clearly understands evaluation metrics (Feasibility, Impact, Innovation)
- Can independently score ideas without seeing other evaluators' scores
- Sees aggregated scores after evaluation period closes
- Provides meaningful feedback on ideas
- Completes evaluations within timeline
- Sees evaluation progress and completion status

**Key Workflows:**
1. Log in and see assigned ideas for evaluation
2. Review idea details and supporting documents
3. Score idea on three metrics (Feasibility, Impact, Innovation) using 1-10 scale
4. Add optional comments/justification for scores
5. Submit evaluation
6. View aggregated scores after evaluation period closes
7. See comparative analysis of all ideas
8. Provide feedback on top-performing ideas

**Motivations:**
- Ensure fair, transparent evaluation process
- Identify truly innovative ideas
- Contribute to organizational innovation
- Maintain credibility and expertise
- See ideas implemented

## Persona 3: David - The Innovation Administrator

**Role:** HR Manager / Innovation Program Manager

**Background:**
David manages the innovation program for the organization. He's responsible for platform administration, campaign management, user management, and ensuring the system runs smoothly. He's tech-savvy and detail-oriented.

**Goals:**
- Create and manage evaluation campaigns
- Assign panel members to campaigns
- Monitor evaluation progress and completion
- Manage user roles and permissions
- Generate reports and analytics
- Ensure system security and data integrity
- Recognize and reward top ideas
- Track innovation metrics and ROI

**Pain Points:**
- Need to manage multiple campaigns and panel assignments
- Concerned about evaluation fairness and consistency
- Wants visibility into evaluation progress
- Needs to generate reports for leadership
- Concerned about system security and access control
- Wants to track implementation of winning ideas

**Technical Proficiency:** High - Comfortable with admin dashboards, configuration, and data management

**Success Criteria:**
- Successfully creates and manages campaigns
- Assigns panel members and tracks assignments
- Monitors evaluation progress in real-time
- Generates comprehensive analytics and reports
- Manages user roles and permissions
- Awards recognition to top 3 ideas
- Maintains system security and audit logs
- Tracks innovation metrics and ROI

**Key Workflows:**
1. Create new evaluation campaign with dates and parameters
2. Assign panel members to campaign
3. Monitor evaluation progress (% complete, panel member status)
4. Close evaluation period and trigger score aggregation
5. View analytics and comparative analysis
6. Identify top 3 ideas automatically
7. Award digital badges to winners
8. Generate reports for leadership
9. Manage user roles and permissions
10. View audit logs and system activity

**Motivations:**
- Successful innovation program execution
- Fair, transparent evaluation process
- High employee participation and engagement
- Successful idea implementation
- Organizational recognition for program success
- Data-driven decision making

## Persona 4: Sarah - The Executive Stakeholder

**Role:** Chief Innovation Officer / Executive Sponsor

**Background:**
Sarah is an executive sponsor of the innovation program. She's interested in high-level metrics, top ideas, and ROI. She uses the platform primarily for reporting and strategic decision-making.

**Goals:**
- See top-performing ideas and innovation trends
- Understand innovation ROI and impact
- Make strategic decisions based on innovation data
- Recognize and celebrate innovation culture
- Track program success metrics
- Identify ideas for implementation

**Pain Points:**
- Needs quick access to key metrics and top ideas
- Wants to understand innovation trends
- Concerned about ROI and implementation rate
- Needs executive-level reporting
- Wants to celebrate innovation publicly

**Technical Proficiency:** Medium - Prefers dashboards and reports over detailed data

**Success Criteria:**
- Quick access to top ideas and key metrics
- Clear understanding of innovation trends
- Visibility into implementation rate
- Executive-level reporting capabilities
- Ability to recognize and celebrate top ideas

**Key Workflows:**
1. View executive dashboard with key metrics
2. See top 10 ideas and innovation trends
3. View implementation rate and ROI
4. Generate executive reports
5. Recognize and celebrate top ideas
6. Make strategic decisions based on data

**Motivations:**
- Successful innovation program
- High ROI and implementation rate
- Strong employee engagement
- Organizational recognition for innovation culture
- Data-driven strategic decisions

## Persona Interaction Map

### Primary Workflows Involving Multiple Personas

**Idea Submission & Evaluation Workflow:**
1. Emma (Employee) submits idea
2. David (Administrator) assigns panel members
3. Marcus (Panel Member) evaluates idea
4. Emma sees evaluation results
5. David awards recognition if top 3
6. Sarah sees top ideas in executive dashboard

**Campaign Management Workflow:**
1. David creates campaign
2. David assigns panel members
3. Marcus and other panel members evaluate
4. David closes evaluation period
5. David generates analytics
6. Sarah reviews results and makes decisions

**Recognition Workflow:**
1. David closes evaluation period
2. System identifies top 3 ideas
3. David awards digital badges
4. Emma receives recognition notification
5. Sarah celebrates top ideas
6. All employees see recognition on leaderboard

---

## Persona-Based Story Organization

Stories will be organized using a hybrid approach:
- **Primary Organization**: Workflow-based (following user journeys)
- **Secondary Organization**: Persona-based grouping within workflows
- **Feature Integration**: Features organized by workflow involvement

This ensures stories capture complete workflows while maintaining clear persona perspectives.
